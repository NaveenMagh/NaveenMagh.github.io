MEGN 301 Mechanical Integration and Design

Group project involving the creation of a portable power generator that can charge devices, display charging status, and have a flashlight fow low-light conditions.

The generator contained a NEMA 23 step motor which charged a series of supercapacitors. The NEMA 23 was cranked using an axle connected to a pulley so that handles on both sides could be used. The power from the motor was converted to DC using a full bridge rectifier which fed into a buck converter. The supercapacitors then powered a 5v 1a USB-C port, an arduino and screen, and a 5W LED.
